01 / Code Work · the heart of the shop

Repairs & Alterations, Built to Code.

An ASME "R" Stamp authorizes welded repairs and alterations to boilers and pressure vessels under the National Board Inspection Code. That is the core of this shop. Qualified procedures, qualified welders, and an Authorized Inspector who signs off before the job is closed.

Pressure Vessel Repair & Re-Rating

Weld repairs, wall and nozzle restoration, and re-rating on pressure vessels, separators, and tanks. Brought back into service to code, not patched.

Boiler Repair & Retubes

Boiler tube replacement, retubes, and pressure-part repair. The work that keeps a boiler legal to fire and safe to run.

Nozzle Additions & Modifications

New nozzles, connection changes, and code alterations welded and documented to NBIC requirements.

Crack Repair & Weld Restoration

Crack excavation and repair, weld build-up, and metal restoration on pressure-retaining parts that have to hold.

03 / Why It Matters

An Uncertified Repair Isn't a Shortcut. It's a Liability.

Welded repairs and alterations to boilers and pressure vessels are governed by code for a reason. The wrong fix from the wrong shop can void your compliance and your insurance. This is why the stamp exists.

/ 01

The "R" Stamp Is the Standard

Repairs and alterations to pressure-retaining equipment are meant to be done by a shop holding a valid National Board "R" Certificate, to NBIC code. We hold it.

/ 02

The Welder Stamps His Own Work

No call center. No mystery subcontractor. The welder who makes your repair is qualified to the procedure and puts his name on it.

/ 03

Every Job Is Documented

Material traceability, qualified procedures, and an R-1 report signed by the Authorized Inspector. Inspection-ready, every time.
